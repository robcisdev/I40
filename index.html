6

What are the main barriers to large-scale digital transformation in Mexican businesses - legacy systems, skills gap, regulation, or cost?

Technical debt continued to be a central concern for companies. Although significant resources have been allocated to modernization, many legacy systems are still hindering organizational agility and slowing growth.

Technical Debt is a Burden to the Integration of GenAI: Incompatibility with Advanced Technologies, Limitation in Agility, Resources Divergence.

For CIOs, addressing technical debt will be a task of strategic vision and pragmatism. The key will be identifying systems whose update is not only critical but also delivers immediate value to the organization, both operationally and from a business perspective.

9

How does nearshoring and the regionalization of supply chains affect technology demands in Mexico?

Nearshoring and the regionalization of supply chains increase the demand for advanced technologies in Mexico. Companies are seeking IT service partners that can offer real-time collaboration, fast decision-making, and access to specialized talent. This demand is driven by the need for:

Agility and Speed: Businesses need to respond quickly to market changes and require IT support that can adapt to these changes in real-time.

Access to Specialized Talent: There's a need for IT professionals with skills in AI, automation, cloud computing, and other advanced technologies.

Innovation: Companies require support to integrate emerging technologies to drive operational efficiencies and achieve measurable business outcomes.

As a result, Mexico's technology demands are shifting towards services that support technology arbitrage, focusing on leveraging technology to drive business value rather than just reducing labor costs.

Security Assessments

Evaluate the security posture of OT systems, identifying vulnerabilities. Penetration testing simulates real-world attacks to test security controls.

Patch Management

Ensuring OT systems are patched with the latest security updates. Patch validation programs test compatibility before deployment.

Unified Monitoring & Incident Response

Advanced threat detection using machine learning and behavioral analysis. Services to help detect, contain, and recover from breaches.

12

What sustainability goals are CIOs pursuing, and how can digital solutions (smart factories, energy efficiency tech) support these?

Key Sustainability Goals: Energy efficiency and carbon footprint reduction. Resource optimization and waste minimization. Compliance with environmental regulations and reporting standards. Circular economy implementation in technology lifecycle.

Digital Enablers: Smart factories with IoT sensors for real-time energy and resource monitoring. AI-driven predictive maintenance to reduce waste and extend equipment life. Digital twins for optimizing operations with minimal environmental impact. Cloud computing migrations to reduce on-premises data center power consumption.

These initiatives create a dual benefit: environmental sustainability and operational cost savings.
